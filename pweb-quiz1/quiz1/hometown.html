Pamekasan

Pamekasan (Hanacaraka: ꦥꦩꦼꦏꦱꦤ꧀, Pegon: ڤامۤكاسان, pronunciation: [paˈməkːasan]) is a district located on the island of Madura. This district is also situated in the East Java province, Indonesia. Its capital is the Pamekasan Sub-district. Pamekasan District is bordered by the Java Sea to the north, the Madura Strait to the south, Sampang District to the west, and Sumenep District to the east. Pamekasan District is the most advanced district in Madura Island in terms of infrastructure and has the lowest poverty rate. Pamekasan District consists of 13 sub-districts, further divided into 178 villages and 11 urban wards. The government center is located in the Pamekasan Sub-district.

In addition to the national official language, Bahasa Indonesia, the widely spoken language in this area is Madurese, particularly the Pamekasan dialect, along with some Javanese speakers. The native inhabitants or ethnic group residing on the island of Madura, including in Pamekasan District, are the Madurese. Nevertheless, there are residents from other ethnicities living in this sub-district, including Javanese, Bawean, Tengger, Osing, Samin, and several other ethnic groups.

Pamekasan District is one of the districts in the Madura region located at the intersection of the Sampang-Sumenep road network. The area of Pamekasan District covers 79,230 hectares, consisting of 13 sub-districts and 189 villages. Broadly speaking, the territory of Pamekasan District consists of lowland in the southern part and highland in the central and northern regions with land slopes not exceeding 2%. Astronomically, Pamekasan District is situated at 6°51'–7°31' South Latitude and 113°19'–113°58' East Longitude. As of the year 2021, the population of Pamekasan Sub-district is 90,212 people, with a population density of 3,298 people per square kilometer. Furthermore, the religious composition in Pamekasan Sub-district is predominantly Islam (99.10%), followed by Christianity (0.84%), consisting of Catholicism (0.43%) and Protestantism (0.41%). A small portion of the population practices Buddhism (0.06%).
