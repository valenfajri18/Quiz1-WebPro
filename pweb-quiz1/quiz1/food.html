Local Foods

Sate Lalat

Sate lalat is not made from flies but rather from small pieces of goat or chicken meat, which are cut into small portions to resemble flies. This specialty from Pamekasan, Madura is lightly grilled, not for an extended period. However, the peanut sauce and soy sauce it's served with make this satay taste delicious.

Campor Lorjuk

Campor Lorjuk bears a resemblance to soto at first glance. Campor Lorjuk consists of slices of rice cake (lontong), cooked vermicelli noodles (mie soun), sweetly seasoned peanuts, sprouts (kecambah goreh), crushed rempeyek, all of which are then drenched with Campor Lorjuk soup. It is also garnished with cooked lorjuk on top as a topping.

Kaldu Kokot

Kaldu Kokot is made from green beans, beef offal, lontong, cassava in the form of cassava croquettes as a side dish, and "kokot" (kikil = the lower part of the cow's leg). 'Kaldu' is the term used for the result of boiling and seasoning the green beans.

Rujak Tajin

Rujak Tajin consists of a creamy porridge made from rice cooked with water, and it is enriched with pieces of beef and pure beef fat. On top of the creamy porridge is sprinkled with a distinctive cucumber pickle known for its yellow color and perfectly tangy taste in the mouth.

Pecong

Pecong is a traditional food consisting of apem, cennil, jungkong, lupis, black glutinous rice, and kolpang, which are sprinkled with grated young coconut and then drizzled with liquid brown sugar. Pecong is served using banana leaves, and uniquely, bamboo sticks are used to skewer Pecong as a substitute for a fork.

Rujak Cingur

Rujak cingur typically consists of slices of various fruits such as cucumber, jicama, young mango, pineapple, ambarella, lontong, tofu, tempeh, bendoyo, cingur, and vegetables like bean sprouts, water spinach, and long beans. All these ingredients are mixed with a sauce or seasoning made from petis and then topped with cingur.
